11 Days 10 Nights

Day 1 : Saturday: Casablanca

Welcome at Mohammed V airport by ATLAS VOYAGES staff. Brief review of the tour during your transfer to the hotel. Check-in, dinner and accommodation. (PS. The hotelâ€™s restaurant closes at 10:30 PM. No possibility dinner served in the room)

Day 2 : Casablanca-Rabat-Tangier

Breakfast and visit the residential district of Anfa, the United Nations Square, the Habous district before going to the cornice area for an (external) visit to the Hassan II Mosque. After lunch an optional lunch (FB basis), continuation to Rabat. Founded in 1150 by the Almohades for military purposes, it became an imperial city at the endof the 18th century under the reign of the Alaouite sultan Mohammed III. Brief visit of the city to discover the Mohamed V mausoleum, an architectural masterpiece. Right next the Hassan Tower with an architecture very close to the Giralda of Seville. You then visit the Kasbah of the Oudayas, classified as World Heritage by UNESCO. Transfer to Train station to enjoy the experience of Train TGV al Bouraq . Arrival to Tangier and transfer to your hotel.

Day 3 : Tangier-Chaouen-Fez

After breakfast, enjoy a panoramic tour of Tangier. Continue to Cape Spartel at theentrance to the Straits of Gibraltar, and see thelighthouse that marks the convergence of theAtlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea. After that, drive to â€˜Blue Pearl cityâ€™ or Chefchaouen city, free time in Chefchaouen famous for its blue- painted walled medina. The blue palette derives from the 15th century, when the town attracted exiles from Spain. Arriving Jewish refugees fleeing the Spanish Inquisition painted their houses blue to reflect their divinity a tradition that continued through the centuries. Enjoy a walking tour through Chefchaouenâ€™s cobbled medina, absorbing the Moorish and Spanish atmosphere & admire the striking blue-and-white houses. Continuation to Fes. Dinner and accommodation at the hotel.

Day 4 : Tuesday: Fez

The whole day is devoted to the city-sightseeing of Fez, the spiritual capital of Morocco: the medieval Medina and the Bou Anania Medersa, the Nejjarine fountain, the Foundouq Nejjarine: Museum of woods, Moulay Idriss mausoleum, and the outside of Karaouine mosque. Optional (FB Basis): Moroccan lunch at a traditional restaurant in the Medina. Afternoon: visit of the esplanade of the Royal Palace with its sumptuous golden door and then sightseeing of Fez Jdid. Dinner and accommodation at the hotel

Day 5 : Wednesday: Fez- Beni Mellal- Marrakech

Departure to Marrakesh passing by the Berber village of Immouzer of Kandar and Ifrane (In case of bad wheather in winter time, itinerary can be modified via Meknes, Adaroch passing by farms and of husbandry cows, Mrirert, Khenifra). Optional (FB Basis): Lunch and a moment of relaxation in Beni Mellal, one of the agricultural centres of Morocco. Dinner and accommodation at the host.

Day 6 : Thursday: Marrakech

Visit of historical Marrakesh famous by its beautiful palaces and monuments: the Bahia Palace, the Koutoubia Minaret and Menara Gardens. Optional lunch in Local restaurant inside the Medina (FB Basis). Afternoon: explore Majorelle Garden with its French-influenced architecture and the intriguing history due to the connection with the iconic French designer Yves Saint Laurent. Complete our sightseeing with a stop at the famous Jamaa El Fna Squarewith its surrounding souks and handicraft quarters Dinner and Accommodation at the hotel.

Day 7 : Friday: Marrakech

Free day at leisure Dinner and accommodation at the hotel. ** The todayâ€™s lunch in not included in the FB supl. **

Day 8 : Saturday: Marrakech- Kasbah Telouet- Kasbah Ait Benhaddou- Ouarzazate

Breakfast and early departure to Ouarzazate by the main road that crosses the Tizi-n- Tichka pass (2260) with a parade of varied and enchanting landscapes and small villages. Visit the sublime Kasbah of Telouet which is on the old caravan route to the Sahara, between Ait Benhaddou and Marraksh. Free time to have lunch. Departure for Ait Benhaddou, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987, is considered one of the best preserved Ksars of all of southrn Morocco. Arrival at Ouarzazate,panoramic visit of the city with stop in front ofthe Kasbah of Taourirt. Dinner and accomodation at the hotel.

Day 9 : Sunday: Ouarzazate-Zagora

After breakfast departure to Taznakth, famous for its Berber carpets. Then continuation southwards to Agdz where we join again the road Ouarzazate-Zagora. From Agdz to Zagora is dotted with palm groves and numerous Kasbahs, most of them falling in ruins, but some are still worth a visit. Freelunch on the way. Dinner and accommodation in Tizi desert (12Km far from Zagora), under desert tents.

Day 10 : Monday : Zagora-Marrakech

Breakfast and then back to Ouarzazate following the same road till Agdz and then through the mountains of the Anti Atlas. Stop at noon in Ouarzazate forlunch. Heading on to Marrakech via the national road crossing the TiznTichka Pass. Arrival in Marrakesh, Dinner and accommodation.

Day 11 : Tuesday : Marrakech-Departure

Transfer to the airport of Marrakesh, assistance with departure formalities
